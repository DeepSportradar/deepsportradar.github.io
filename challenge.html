ACM MMSports 2022

5th International ACM Workshop on Multimedia Content Analysis in Sports

Challenges

This year, MMSports proposes a competition where participants will compete over State-of-the-art problems applied to real-world sport specific data. The competition is made of 4 individual challenges, each of which is sponsored by SportRadar with a US$1,000.00 prize. Each challenge comes with a toolkit describing the task, the dataset and metrics on which participants will be evaluated:

  • Ball 3D localization
  • Camera calibration
  • Instance segmentation
  • Players re-identification

The challenges are hosted on EvalAI where participants will submit the prediction of their model on an evaluation set for which labels are kept secret.

Datasets

Each challenge is based on a dataset of manually annotated images made publicly available by SportRadar. The proposed solutions must not use any other data than the data specifically given in the challenge toolkit. However, pretraining on public dataset is accepted as long as the pretrained model comes from a well known model-zoo.

Awards

The challenges will run in two phases. In the first phase, participants compete to have their method published in the conference proceedings. For the second phase, participants compete for a US$500.00 prize per challenge. Participants are allowed (and encoraged) to submit to both phases, and can compete in the 4 different challenges. In addition, for each challenge, a jury prize of US$500.00 will be awarded to the team proposing the most innovative solution, regardless of the performance on the leaderboard.

First phase - Compete to have your method published in the conference proceedings

For each challenge, the teams performing best on the leaderboard will have a 2 weeks delay to write a workshop paper that will benefit from a fast reviewing process.

Second phase - Compete to win up to US$1,000.00

For each challenge, the teams performing best on the leaderboard will be awarded a US$500.00 prize. In addition, To be eligible, submissions should be acompagnied with a technical report describing the proposed approach and the code to reproduce their results. Reports are to be uploaded to ArXiv and the code to a public repository. The associated links must be provided within the final EvalAI submission. The reports and the code will be reviewed by the challenge organizers that may reject any submission for which legitimity is uncertain.

Jury prize - Propose the most innovative solution to win a US$500.00 prize

An additional prize will be awarded to the most innovative solution
